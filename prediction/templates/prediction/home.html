About Heart Disease

Understand the risk factors and types

💓

What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease (cardiovascular disease) refers to a range of conditions affecting the heart's structure and function, including narrowed or blocked blood vessels, irregular heartbeats, and structural abnormalities. It is the leading cause of death globally, responsible for over 17 million deaths each year.

🔬

How Does It Occur?

Plaque (cholesterol, fat, calcium) builds up inside arteries over time — a process called atherosclerosis. This narrows arteries, restricts blood flow, and can trigger heart attacks or strokes. Risk increases with age, genetics, and lifestyle choices.

🚬

Lifestyle-Induced (Preventable)

  • Smoking & tobacco use
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • High-fat, high-sodium junk food diet
  • Physical inactivity / sedentary lifestyle
  • Chronic stress & poor sleep
  • Obesity & Type-2 Diabetes
Preventable Modifiable Risk
🧬

Natural / Physical Conditions

  • Genetics & family history
  • Advancing age (risk ↑ after 45)
  • Congenital heart defects (from birth)
  • Hypertension (high blood pressure)
  • High LDL cholesterol levels
  • Hormonal changes (e.g., post-menopause)
Non-Modifiable Genetic
⚡

Types of Heart Disease

  • Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) – Blocked arteries
  • Arrhythmia – Irregular heartbeat
  • Heart Failure – Weakened pumping
  • Valve Disease – Defective heart valves
  • Congenital HD – Birth defects
  • Cardiomyopathy – Disease of heart muscle
🚨

Warning Signs

  • Chest pain or pressure (angina)
  • Shortness of breath
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Fatigue and dizziness
  • Swelling in legs/feet
  • Pain radiating to arm/jaw/back
Seek medical help immediately
